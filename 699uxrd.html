LINQ: Monitoring Your Child's "Data-day" Activities and Progress


Overview

A UX Research and Design mastery partner project that took place over a span of 3 months (Sept—Dec 2017). The project demonstrates knowledge in understanding and application of UX research methods, analysis and synthesis of research data, and iteration of designs through informed decisions.


Individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) struggle with social interactions, communication, and sensory processing. The disorder complicates daily life and causes financial strain. As of 2015, 1 in 45 children between the ages of 3—17 were diagnosed.


Below highlights the research + design process and a proposed system aimed to provide hassle-free longitudinal monitoring and documentation.

Observation and Motivation

How it all started

My peers and I entered a proposal for Games User Research Summit 2017 on a wearable system involving haptic feedback that will promote interactions between special needs children and an open form of communication between parents and their [hypo-sensitive] children. We spent our spring break that year in San Francisco sharing our vision of providing parents a glimps of their children's lives through a poster presentation and engaging with GUR Summit attendees.


**This project is a continuation of that vision.

Insights

Generated from 7 surveys from parents, 2 expert interviews, and 9+ literature reviews


A list of design objectives


  • Parents desire a way to monitor their children’s progress as one of their primary concern is their children’s lack of socialization
  • Current methods of monitoring are anecdotal, biased, and qualitative [1]
  • Evidence-based care is essential in monitoring education and healthcare disabilities [2]
  • ASD is very individualized and there is no one size fits all
  • Data recording allows parents to be more in the loop while keeping the documentation burden off of educators and other caregivers

System Design

Key Features

A closer look at the dashboard homepage


Web App Features

  • Dashboard: allows for data monitoring based on different interaction metrics and amount of data, highlights trends, and allows for PDF file downloads. Our main design solution based on the needs voiced by caretakers to be more aware of their children’s condition.
  • Profiles: allows account holder to register more than one users on the platform as we found that it is common for family to have more than one children on the spectrum as the disorder is a genetic condition.
  • Devices: allows for registration and modification of devices, shows status of device, and allows for data toggle between devices. Because of our limited contact with our target audience, we made our best assumption that devices used vary as do their symptoms and needs.
  • History: shows all download history on the account to allow users to revisit previously downloaded content, providing good records keeping and alleviates that additional burden on the parents.
